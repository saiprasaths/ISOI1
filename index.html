MALARIA DETECTION USING TRANSFER LEARNING

TRANSFER LEARNING

The main objective of this project is to give patients the instant result whether they have malaria or not. Our product is easy to use and does not require any medical knowledge therefore can be used by anyone. It will also ensure contactless checkup during this COVID-19 pandemic and reduce some workload for the doctors.Using transfer learning models such as (VGG16, VGG19, Inception etc.) and adequate dataset, the accuracy of prediction can be boosted to a great extent and reducing the timing of medical tests to a matter of seconds. This project could be a great step to control a disease like malaria.

Health-Monitoring-System

This project describes the construction and design of a simple low cost IoT and GSM based healthcare monitoring system at home. This framework consists of various smart sensors like pulse oximeter heart rate sensor (MAX30100) to measure patients SPO2 and pulse reading, IR temperature sensor (MLX90614) to measure body temperature and also equipped with a GSM module to send SMS (short message service) to doctors when body vitals are above or below normal level so that the doctors can take emergency measures immediately. The system is also equipped with a ventilator for use in emergency purposes when the patient's SPO2 levels are low. This can be used without any medical help. This system uses NodeMCU as a microcontroller board with Google Firebase as an online database to keep a track of patient’s vitals on a real-time basis. These data can be later accessed by doctors through laptops and smartphones. This system will help bridge gaps between the patients and doctors during the pandemic situation.

  • Link: https://github.com/cs28-droid/Health-Monitoring-System
    •
  • Category: HARDWARE

Covid-19 Healthcare Bot

A bot that can record the real-time temperature and SP02 of every individual and send it to the doctors, so that they can filtrate the people who need to go for the second level of testing for COVID-19.Based on real-time IoT technology.Equipped with a system that follows both hospital lines and also light.The overall prototype of the bot with the chassis, sensors and sub-systems costs about $55 (₹4000).
